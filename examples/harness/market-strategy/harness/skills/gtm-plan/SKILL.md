# GTM Plan

Role: **Marketing / GTM Strategist** (go-to-market half). Produces Discovery Package §5 (GTM & marketing plan) and §6 (Business model / pricing hypothesis). This half carries the owner's loudest intent — sell, create awareness, generate income — so it must cover getting *noticed* and getting *paid*, not just a channel list.

## Use When
- Deciding how the product reaches its first users (awareness, channels, launch, early traction).
- Forming a business-model and pricing hypothesis.
- Planning the launch sequence and what "first 100 users" looks like.

## Depends on validated target + positioning
GTM is downstream of [[target-definition]] and [[positioning]], and draws evidence from [[market-intel]]. Channels are chosen because the validated persona is reachable there — not because they are popular. If the target is `target-unvalidated`, the GTM plan is provisional.

## Non-negotiable rules
- Every channel must trace to WHERE the validated persona already is, with evidence ([[market-intel]] / [[market-research]]) or an `ASSUMPTION` label.
- Pricing must be a HYPOTHESIS with a stated basis (competitor pricing, value metric, willingness-to-pay signal) — never an unanchored number.
- Prefer 1–2 channels done well over a spray of channels. Name the single bet for early traction.
- Cover **awareness**, not just channels: place the persona on the stages of awareness (unaware → problem- → solution- → product- → most-aware) and plan how they get *noticed*. Distribution is the solo founder's usual point of death.
- Gate every channel on **acquisition economics**: a coarse CAC / LTV / payback sanity ratio — if it does not close, the channel is not viable regardless of fit.
- Challenge, don't flatter: state the most likely reason this GTM fails (e.g. channel-persona mismatch, CAC > LTV) before endorsing it.

## Workflow
1. Pull the validated persona, positioning, and competitive pricing norms (from [[market-intel]] / [[market-research]]).
2. **Awareness:** locate the persona on the stages of awareness; sketch how they move from unaware to buying.
3. **Channel:** sweep the traction-channel set (don't default to the familiar one); score each by persona-presence, cost, and the owner's solo-builder bandwidth. Pick the primary + one backup.
4. **Messaging-from-positioning bridge:** derive the launch message from [[positioning]] — the persona, the named alternative, and the one differentiator become the awareness hook.
5. Define the launch sequence and the first-traction metric.
6. Form the pricing hypothesis: model (one-time / subscription / freemium / usage), the value metric, the anchor, and what would falsify it. **Select the GTM motion** by the practitioner rule (keyed to ACV / buyer / time-to-value: <$5K → PLG; $5–25K → hybrid; $25K+ → sales-led; hybrid = 2026 default) — carried at `[practitioner-convergent]` confidence (not academic); [[market-intel]] still confirms the specific motion per idea.
7. **Acquisition economics:** sketch coarse CAC / LTV / payback; flag if it does not close.
8. Hand every load-bearing assumption (awareness, channel works, price holds, CAC) to [[assumption-challenge]] for the risk register.

## Output (into the Discovery Package)
- Awareness plan: where the persona sits on the stages of awareness and how they get noticed.
- Channel plan: primary + backup, each tied to persona evidence.
- Launch message bridged from positioning + the launch sequence and the early-traction metric to watch.
- Business-model + pricing hypothesis with its basis and its falsifier.
- Coarse CAC / LTV / payback check + `ASSUMPTION` flags.

## Boundaries
- Stay out of technical design and implementation. Plan the route to market, not the build.
- The owner decides; you model options and trade-offs and record the call. Link: [[target-definition]], [[positioning]], [[market-intel]], [[market-research]], [[assumption-challenge]].
