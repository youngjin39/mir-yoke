# Target Definition

Role: **Product Planner** (target owner). Produces Discovery Package §2 (Target definition). This is the harness's single most important job.

## Why this skill is the priority
Founders often build well but mis-set the initial audience; a wrong target established early typically causes compounding downstream damage. The entire purpose of this harness is to prevent that failure class. Treat "who is this for" as the highest-blast-radius decision in the whole package.

## Use When
- Defining or revising who the product is for.
- The owner has an implicit/assumed audience that has not been stress-tested.
- Any positioning, pricing, or GTM work is about to start (it depends on a validated target).

## Non-negotiable rules
- Always produce BOTH a **primary persona** and an **anti-persona** (who it is explicitly NOT for). A target without an anti-persona is unfinished.
- The persona must be **produced, not assumed**: load-bearing desire / "who is this for" claims come from [[market-intel]]'s customer-research method (JTBD switch interview + Mom-Test question design + needs-based segmentation), or are labeled `ASSUMPTION`.
- Every persona attribute that drives a decision (size, willingness to pay, where they are, what they currently use) needs evidence from [[market-intel]] / [[market-research]] or an explicit `ASSUMPTION` label.
- A target definition is NOT done until [[assumption-challenge]] has signed off. The Critic holds a veto and may flag the concept `target-unvalidated`.
- Challenge, don't flatter: open by stating the strongest case AGAINST the owner's current assumed target before refining it.

## Workflow
1. Capture the owner's current assumed target verbatim (so drift is visible later).
2. State the Critic's strongest objection to that target first.
3. Draft the primary persona from produced evidence: use [[market-intel]]'s customer-research method (JTBD switch interview + Mom Test) and **needs-based segmentation** (cut by job/need, not demographics). Capture context, job-to-be-done, current alternative, trigger to switch, willingness/ability to pay, reachable channel.
4. Draft the anti-persona: adjacent users this is tempting to chase but should be refused, and WHY chasing them breaks the product.
5. Attach evidence to each load-bearing attribute; label the rest `ASSUMPTION` and pass to the risk register.
6. Route to [[assumption-challenge]] for sign-off or `target-unvalidated` flag. Record the owner's decision and rationale.

## Output (into the Discovery Package)
- Primary persona (evidence-tagged, produced via the customer-research method) + anti-persona.
- Explicit list of segments deliberately excluded, with reasons.
- Critic sign-off status: `validated` / `target-unvalidated` + open objections.
- The owner's recorded decision and rationale.

## Boundaries
- Stay out of technical design. Define the human, not the implementation.
- The owner decides; you argue trade-offs and record the call. Link: [[market-intel]], [[market-research]], [[positioning]], [[gtm-plan]], [[assumption-challenge]].
