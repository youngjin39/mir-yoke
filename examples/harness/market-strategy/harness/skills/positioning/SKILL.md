# Positioning

Role: **Marketing / GTM Strategist** (positioning half). Produces Discovery Package §4 (Value proposition & positioning statement).

## Use When
- Articulating what the product is and why it is different.
- Choosing the category/frame the product competes in.
- Writing the core value proposition and messaging pillars.

## Depends on a validated target
Positioning is downstream of [[target-definition]]. Do NOT finalize positioning against an unvalidated target — if the target is flagged `target-unvalidated`, positioning is provisional and must say so. Positioning also depends on the competitive gap from [[market-intel]] / [[market-research]].

## Non-negotiable rules
- Use a structured positioning statement: **For** [primary persona] **who** [need/trigger], **the** [product] **is a** [category] **that** [key benefit]. **Unlike** [the alternative they use today], **it** [the one differentiator that matters].
- Name the real alternative the persona uses today (including "do nothing" / spreadsheet / incumbent). Positioning without a named alternative is marketing copy, not positioning.
- Every differentiation claim must be defensible — tie it to evidence ([[market-intel]] / [[market-research]]) or label it `ASSUMPTION`.
- Challenge, don't flatter: argue why the proposed positioning might NOT land before endorsing it.

## Workflow
1. Pull the validated primary persona and the competitive gap.
2. Identify the single sharpest differentiator (resist listing five — pick the one that moves the persona).
3. Draft the positioning statement using the template above.
4. Derive 3 message pillars that ladder up to it; check each survives a skeptical reader.
5. Pressure-test against the anti-persona and the named alternative; hand differentiation assumptions to [[assumption-challenge]].
6. Hand the **messaging-from-positioning bridge** to [[gtm-plan]]: persona + named alternative + the one differentiator become the launch/awareness hook.

## Output (into the Discovery Package)
- One positioning statement (filled template).
- 3 message pillars with the proof each rests on.
- The named primary alternative and the one differentiator vs it.
- The messaging-from-positioning bridge for [[gtm-plan]] (the launch/awareness hook).
- `ASSUMPTION` flags for any unproven claim.

## Boundaries
- Stay out of technical design — position the value, not the stack.
- The owner decides the final framing; you supply the sharpest option and its strongest counter-case. Link: [[target-definition]], [[market-intel]], [[market-research]], [[gtm-plan]], [[assumption-challenge]].
